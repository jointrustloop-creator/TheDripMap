# Price Index Link-Building / Digital-PR Kit

**Purpose:** earn the Canadian backlinks that move commercial money terms
("iv therapy toronto", pos ~78) toward page 1. Content and on-page SEO are
already strong (informational pages rank pos 6-10); the missing ingredient is
off-site authority. The IV Price Index is the linkable asset: unique data no
competitor in Canada publishes. Journalists, bloggers, and association pages
link to original data.

**The linkable asset (send people here):** https://www.thedripmap.com/iv-prices
Plus city pages: /iv-prices/toronto, /iv-prices/calgary, /iv-prices/edmonton

---

## 1. The data story (headline stats, June 2026, CAD)

Use these real figures verbatim; each links back to a page that proves it.

- **A standard IV vitamin drip in Canada runs a median of about CA$175.**
- **Toronto:** median CA$175, range CA$119-$399 across 17 clinics. NAD+ median
  CA$250; a beauty/glow drip runs up to CA$470.
- **Calgary:** median CA$200, range CA$75-$260 across 6 clinics.
- **Edmonton:** median CA$150, range CA$75-$295 across 8 clinics.
- **The spread is the story:** the same "standard drip" ranges from CA$75 to
  CA$399 depending on the clinic. Prices are rarely posted, so patients can't
  comparison-shop. TheDripMap is the first to aggregate and publish them.

**Angles that get picked up:**
1. "What IV therapy actually costs in Canada" (consumer/personal-finance desks).
2. "IV drips cost 5x more at some clinics than others" (the price-spread hook).
3. City angle: "How much IV therapy costs in Toronto vs Calgary vs Edmonton."
4. Wellness-spending trend piece with hard local numbers.

## 2. Target link sources (Canada-first, ranked by ease)

**Tier 1 - easiest, do first:**
- HARO / Qwoted / Help a B2B Writer: answer journalist queries on wellness,
  cost-of-living, health spending. Cite the Price Index figures. Free.
- Local-news tip lines / city desks (Toronto Star, CTV, Global, CBC local,
  Narcity, blogTO, Daily Hive Calgary/Edmonton): pitch the data story.
- Reddit/forum answers where useful (no spam; link the data page when it
  genuinely answers "how much is IV therapy in {city}").

**Tier 2 - resource-page + association links:**
- Provincial nursing / naturopathic association public resource pages (they
  sometimes link consumer-education pages on IV therapy safety + rules). Pitch
  the "who can legally give IV in Canada" guide, not a commercial page.
- Wellness / health directories and "best of {city}" roundups.
- University/college student-wellness pages (cost-of-living, health resources).

**Tier 3 - partner links (owned):**
- Every claimed clinic: ask for a "Find us on TheDripMap" link in their site
  footer or "as featured on" section. Bundle it into the Safety Verified badge
  kit so it is a natural part of onboarding. 20 claimed clinics = 20 potential
  relevant links.

## 4. How to track it
- Watch Referring Domains in GSC (Links report) + Ahrefs/Search Console.
- Re-pull GSC in 30/60/90 days; the money-term positions (iv therapy toronto
  etc.) are the KPI. Even 10-15 relevant Canadian links should start moving
  the whole commercial cluster, because they all share the domain authority.

## 5. Keep the asset fresh (compounds the links)
Add cities to the Price Index as coverage grows (n>=3 clinics with prices).
Each new city = a new citable stat = a new pitch angle for that city's media.
Owner-verified prices from the finish form fold in automatically. A dated
"2026 vs 2027" update is itself a story next year.
