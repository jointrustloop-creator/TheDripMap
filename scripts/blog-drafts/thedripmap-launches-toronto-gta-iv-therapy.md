## The Safety Verified difference

Most patients don't know what to look for when evaluating an IV therapy clinic. In Ontario the basics are:

- **A registered nurse or nurse practitioner** administering the infusion, in good standing with the CNO. Aestheticians, technicians, and unlicensed staff are not authorized to start an IV.
- **A medical director or supervising physician** with documented oversight of the clinic's protocols and patient assessment process.
- **A good-faith examination** before treatment. For new patients this typically means an intake form, brief health history, and either a virtual or in-person review with a clinician before a needle is placed.
- **Clean, dedicated infusion space** with appropriate emergency equipment, sterile technique, and proper handling of pharmaceutical-grade ingredients.
- **Transparent pricing and ingredient lists.** If a clinic can't or won't tell you exactly what's in your bag and what each component costs, that's a signal.

The Safety Verified badge on TheDripMap is our shorthand for "this clinic has demonstrated the basics above." It's not a marketing label. Clinics that have not been verified do not carry it, and we list them honestly — without the badge — so patients can make informed choices either way.

This matters especially in Ontario, where the regulatory bar is higher than in many US markets and where the consequences of non-compliance are real. Patients deserve to know which side of that line a clinic sits on before they book.
