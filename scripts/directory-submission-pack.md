## 3. Submission checklist — top 10 directories

Sequence: do the Citations Sprint first (10 mins each, low risk), then the SaaS/Founder Sprint (15-20 mins each), then the vertical match (Wellness.com).

### Citations Sprint (~60 minutes total)

- [ ] **1. YellowPages.ca / Canada411** — propagates to multiple Canadian directories
  - URL: <https://solutions.yp.ca/free-online-listing>
  - Account required: Yes (free)
  - Fields: Business name, Toronto address, phone, category (Health & Wellness), website, description (use 160-char), hours (24/7 online service)
  - Time: 5-10 min

- [ ] **2. Yelp for Business** — DA 93
  - URL: <https://biz.yelp.com/claim>
  - Account required: Yes (free)
  - Fields: Business name, address, phone, category (`Internet Service Providers` → `Health Information Service` or similar), hours, photos, website, hours
  - Time: 10-15 min

- [ ] **3. Foursquare for Business** — DA 92, syndicates to Apple Maps + many POI APIs
  - URL: <https://business.foursquare.com/claim/>
  - Account required: Yes (free)
  - Fields: Business name, address, category, hours, photos, website
  - Time: 10 min

- [ ] **4. Better Business Bureau** (free, non-accredited listing)
  - URL: <https://www.bbb.org/get-listed>
  - Account required: Yes (free, no accreditation fee needed)
  - Fields: Business name, address, phone, email, website, up to 3 categories
  - Time: 10-15 min
  - Note: Link is `nofollow` but trust signal is high

- [ ] **5. Manta** — DA 76, US-only
  - URL: <https://www.manta.com/business-listings/add-your-company>
  - Account required: Yes (free)
  - Fields: Business name, US address (or HQ partner city), phone, category, hours, website, 300-word description
  - Time: 10 min
  - Note: Even though TheDripMap is Toronto-based, listing a US-relevant city or "online service" works

- [ ] **6. Hotfrog** — DA 70, international citation
  - URL: <https://www.hotfrog.com/> → "Add Business" in footer
  - Account required: Yes (email verification)
  - Fields: Email, company name, address (pinned on map), category, description, website
  - Time: 10 min

### Founder / SaaS Sprint (~60 minutes total)

- [ ] **7. Crunchbase** — DA 91, standard founder hygiene
  - URL: <https://www.crunchbase.com/add-new> (after login)
  - Account required: Yes (email + social auth verification)
  - Fields: Company name, 300-word description, website, HQ city (Toronto), founded date (2026), categories (Health, Online Directory, Marketplaces), employee count (1-10), logo, founder profile
  - Time: 20 min (plus a few hours for verification)

- [ ] **8. AlternativeTo** — DA 85, dofollow backlink, great for "alternative to X" long-tail SEO
  - URL: <https://alternativeto.net/> → "Add software" after sign-in
  - Account required: Yes (free)
  - Fields: App name, 300-word description, platforms (Web/Online), category (Health Information / Directory), logo, screenshots, website, link 2-3 alternatives (try: "Drip Hydration", "Mobile IV Medics", "Hydrate Medical")
  - Time: 15-20 min

- [ ] **9. SaaSHub** — DA 78, dofollow, slow moderation but worth waiting
  - URL: <https://www.saashub.com/services/submit>
  - Account required: Yes (free)
  - Fields: Product name, 300-word description, screenshots (3-5), founder story, category, website
  - Time: 15 min (moderation can take 1-4 weeks)

### Vertical match (~20 minutes)

- [ ] **10. Wellness.com** — DA 67, vertical match perfect for IV/wellness
  - URL: <https://www.wellness.com/secure/provider/public/add>
  - Account required: Yes (free basic listing)
  - Fields: Practice name, category (closest fit: `Wellness Center` or list as resource), address, phone, website, hours, services list, payment methods, optional photos
  - Time: 15-20 min

### Outreach (not a form submission)

- [ ] **11. American IV Association** — Niche but exact-fit audience
  - Action: Email `info@americaniv.com` requesting inclusion as a free editorial/resource link
  - Suggested subject: `Resource link request — TheDripMap.com IV therapy directory`
  - Suggested body: brief intro, link, value-prop (their members benefit from free directory exposure)
  - Time: 10 min
  - Do NOT pay the $396/year membership unless TheDripMap revenue justifies it

## 6. Indie Hackers milestone post

**Format:** A narrative milestone post, not a directory listing. Use the "Milestones" or general post format. Link to the product page at the bottom.

### Title options

- `How I built TheDripMap — a directory of 600+ IV therapy clinics across North America`
- `Building TheDripMap: lessons from 6 months of cataloguing the IV therapy market`
- `Why I'm building TheDripMap — the IV therapy market needed a real directory`

### Post body

> ## Why I built TheDripMap
>
> The wellness IV therapy market has been one of the fastest-growing health categories in North America over the past few years. By 2026, it's a $3B+ global market — new clinics opening in every major metro, mobile providers serving hotels and homes 24/7, NAD+ protocols hitting $1,000+ per session.
>
> But for patients, the experience of finding the right clinic was awful. Yelp listings are sparse. Google Maps shows you nearby clinics but tells you nothing about what's actually in their drips or what they charge. Reddit threads from 2022 are still ranking for "IV therapy [city]" queries despite being completely out of date. Each clinic's own website is, naturally, marketing — every drip "transforms" you, every NAD+ session is "life-changing."
>
> I built TheDripMap as the directory the market needed: 600+ verified clinics across the US and Canada, comparable by city / treatment / price / mobile availability, plus 85+ patient-focused guides written without marketing inflation.
>
> ## What's in it now
>
> - 600+ IV therapy clinic listings across major US cities (NYC, LA, Vegas, SF, Chicago, Houston, DC, Boston, Phoenix, Atlanta, Dallas, Seattle, San Diego, Philly, Denver, Miami, and more) plus the Canadian GTA (Toronto, Mississauga, Oakville, Vaughan, Richmond Hill)
> - Treatment-specific landing pages for the 10 most common drip protocols
> - State / province directories
> - 85+ blog posts covering city guides, treatment deep-dives, cost breakdowns, comparison content, and safety
> - 60-second matching quiz that takes patient goals + location + budget → best-fit clinic
> - Free "claim your listing" flow for clinic operators with token-based email verification
>
> ## What I learned building it
>
> 1. **The supply side is harder than the demand side.** Building a quality clinic dataset took 10x the effort of building the UI. Sourcing, deduplicating, normalizing, and verifying clinic information across 600+ providers required real research time.
>
> 2. **Content is the moat.** The directory itself is replicable; the trust-building content is much harder. Writing 85 patient-focused guides took weeks. I think that's where the long-term defensibility lives.
>
> 3. **SEO is everything for directories.** Every architectural decision (URL structure, sitemap design, schema markup, metadata generation, ISR vs SSG) was driven by "will Googlebot understand and rank this properly?" The Next.js App Router worked well for this once I figured out the patterns.
>
> 4. **Mobile IV is way bigger than I expected.** Every research conversation, every clinic interview, every market overview kept pointing to the mobile model as the fastest-growing segment. Built mobile-availability filtering as a first-class feature based on this.
>
> ## What's next
>
> - Expanding clinic coverage in Florida (Miami, Tampa, Orlando), Texas (Austin, San Antonio), and major Canadian metros (Vancouver, Calgary, Montreal)
> - Booking integration with claimed clinics
> - Patient review/rating layer
> - Partnerships with IV therapy professional associations
>
> If you've used IV therapy and have opinions about what was missing from your booking experience, I'd love to hear them.
>
> 🔗 https://www.thedripmap.com
